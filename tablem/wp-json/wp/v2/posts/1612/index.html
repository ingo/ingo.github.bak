Ladyfingers<\/h3>
  • Servings: <\/strong>24-36<\/li>
  • Print<\/a><\/li><\/ul>
    <\/p>\n\n\n\n

    Ingredients<\/h4><\/p>\n\n\n\n
    • 3 Eggs, separated<\/li>
    • 6 tbs granulated sugar<\/li>
    • 3\/4 c cake flour, sifted<\/li>
    • 6 tbs Confectioner’s Sugar<\/li><\/ul>\n\n\n\n

      <\/div>\n\n\n\n

      Directions<\/h4><\/p>\n\n\n\n
      1. Preheat the oven to 350 degrees, then brush 2 baking sheets with softened butter and line with parchment paper. A Silpat works well too.<\/li>
      2. Beat the egg whites with a whisk or electric mixer until stiff peaks form. Gradually beat in the granulated sugar and continue beating until stiff peaks form again; the meringue will be glossy and smooth. Lightly beat the egg yolks with a fork, and fold into the meringue with a wooden spoon. Sift the flour over the mixture and fold in gently.<\/li>
      3. Pipe the ladyfingers: Fit the pastry bag with the large plain tip and fill with the ladyfinger batter. Pipe strips of batter 5 in. long and 3\/4 in. wide diagonally onto the baking sheets, leaving 1 to 2 in. between each strip. Sprinkle half the confectioner’s sugar over the ladyfingers; wait 5 minutes and sprinkle with the remaining sugar.<\/li>
      4. Holding the parchment paper in place with your thumb, lift one side of the baking sheet and gently tap it on the work surface to remove excess confectioner’s sugar.<\/li>
      5. Bake without opening the oven door for 10 min.<\/li>
      6. Rotate the baking sheet so the ladyfingers color evenly. Cook until lightly golden, about 5 more minutes.<\/li>
      7. Remove the ladyfingers from the baking sheet with a metal spatula while still hot, and let cool on a rack.<\/li><\/ol>\n\n\n\n
