As a young kid, Ingo would often make this pancake recipe. It’s so named as it came out of the “Mickey Mouse Cookbook.”<\/span><\/p>\n\n\n\n

Mickey Mouse Pancakes<\/h3>
  • Servings: <\/strong>4<\/li>
  • Print<\/a><\/li><\/ul>
    <\/p>\n\n\n\n

    It is not necessary to shape the pancakes like Mickey Mouse’s head, but it helps.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

    Ingredients<\/h4><\/p>\n\n\n\n
    • 1 cup flour, all-purpose, unbleached<\/li>
    • 1 tsp. baking powder<\/li>
    • 1\/2 tsp. baking soda<\/li>
    • 1 tbsp. sugar<\/li>
    • 1\/4 tsp. salt<\/li>
    • 1 egg<\/li>
    • 1 cup milk<\/li>
    • 1 tbsp. vegetable oil<\/li><\/ul>\n\n\n\n

      <\/div><\/p>\n\n\n\n

      Directions<\/h4><\/p>\n\n\n\n
      1. Combine all dry ingredients in one bowl. Combine wet ingredients in a separate bowl and then mix into dry ingredients. Cook on a griddle over medium-high heat.<\/li><\/ol>\n\n\n\n
