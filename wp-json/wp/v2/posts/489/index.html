“I don’t like carrot pur\u00e9e,” says Jane. “Why not?” I say. “I just don’t.” “Okay. I’ll make some for myself and regular carrots for you”. I go off and cook dinner. I’m determined to make a carrot pur\u00e9e she’ll like. Rosemary chicken, with roast asparagus and the carrot pur\u00e9e on the side. “So how’s the carrot pur\u00e9e?” I ask. Jane concedes, blushing, “It’s pretty good. Are you going to publish the recipe?” <\/span><\/p>\n\n\n\n

Mint-Scented Carrot-Fennel Pur\u00e9e<\/h3>
  • Servings: <\/strong>4<\/li>
  • Print<\/a><\/li><\/ul>
    <\/p>\n\n\n\n

    Admittedly, this isn’t just carrot pur\u00e9e, but it’s mostly carrots. Adjust the amount of butter and cream to suit, or substitute with milk if you’re feeling health-conscious.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

    Ingredients<\/h4><\/p>\n\n\n\n
    • 3\/4 lb. carrots<\/li>
    • 1 fennel bulb<\/li>
    • 5 mint leaves<\/li>
    • 1 cup chicken or vegetable stock<\/li>
    • 1 tbsp. butter<\/li>
    • 2 tbsp. heavy cream or milk<\/li>
    • 1\/2 tsp. salt<\/li>
    • pepper<\/li><\/ul>\n\n\n\n

      <\/div><\/p>\n\n\n\n

      Directions<\/h4><\/p>\n\n\n\n
      1. Peel the carrots and chop roughly.<\/li>
      2. Cut the top off the fennel bulb, remove the very bottom off the base, slice in half, remove the tough inner core, and chop roughly.<\/li>
      3. Place the carrots, fennel, mint, stock and salt in a two-quart saucepan over medium heat.<\/li>
      4. Simmer until vegetables are very soft, 20-25 minutes.<\/li>
      5. Remove pot from heat.<\/li>
      6. Add butter and cream and pur\u00e9e with an immersion blender, food processor or blender.<\/li>
      7. Adjust salt and pepper to taste and serve.<\/li>
      8. This may be prepared a day ahead of time. Warm in the oven or microwave.<\/li><\/ol>\n\n\n\n
