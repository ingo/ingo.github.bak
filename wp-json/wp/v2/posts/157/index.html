Baba Ghanouj can be purchased in the store, but the fresh version is much tastier.<\/span>
\n<\/p>\n

Yield<\/h3>\n

2 cups<\/p>\n

Ingredients<\/h3>\n
    \n
  • 2 lbs. eggplants<\/li>\n
  • 3 cloves garlic<\/li>\n
  • Salt<\/li>\n
  • 4 tbsp. tahina (sesame paste)<\/li>\n
  • Juice of two lemons<\/li>\n
  • 1\/2 tsp. ground cumin<\/li>\n
  • Olive oil<\/li>\n<\/ul>\n

    Directions<\/h3>\n

    Heat the broiler. Broil the eggplants (be sure to pierce the eggplant in a few places before broiling to avoid eggplant explosions) until charred on all sides and very soft. Remove and place in a paper bag. Once cool, remove skin and mash in colander with a folk, allowing extra juice to seep away. Pour eggplant into a mixing bowl and add garlic, a pinch of salt, tahina and lemon juice. Adjust flavoring to taste.<\/p>\n

    Place mixture in shallow bowl and lightly drizzle with olive oil. This is best served lukewarm.<\/p>\n","protected":false},"excerpt":{"rendered":"
