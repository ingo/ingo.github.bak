This is a light, Asian influenced salad dressing. It works equally well spilled onto a simple bed of lettuce leaves, or a more complex salad. Salad\u00a0lettuces are especially fresh and abundant in the summer –thus the name.<\/span><\/p>\n\n\n\n

Black Sesame Summer Dressing<\/h3>
  • Servings: <\/strong>1 cup<\/li>
  • Print<\/a><\/li><\/ul>
    <\/p>\n\n\n\n

    This dressing works well on a very simple salad, like the Boston lettuce shown here.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

    Ingredients<\/h4><\/p>\n\n\n\n
    • 1 shallot<\/li>
    • 1\/3 cup seasoned rice vinegar<\/li>
    • 2\/3 cup seasoned canola oil<\/li>
    • 1 tsp. toasted sesame oil<\/li>
    • 1 tbsp. black sesame seeds<\/li>
    • salt<\/li>
    • pepper<\/li><\/ul>\n\n\n\n

      <\/div><\/p>\n\n\n\n

      Directions<\/h4><\/p>\n\n\n\n
      1. Peel and mince the shallot and place in the bottom of a large bowl.<\/li>
      2. Add rice vinegar and let site 10 minutes.<\/li>
      3. Whisk in oils until emulsified, and then add sesame seeds. Season to taste with salt and pepper. <\/li><\/ol>\n\n\n\n
