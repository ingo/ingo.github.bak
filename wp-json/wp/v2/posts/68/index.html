An Italian specialty, especially good when prepared on the grill. This requires some heavy object to weigh the chicken down. A cast-iron frying pan, or several bricks placed on top of an old jelly-roll pan, works well.<\/span>
\n<\/p>\n

Yield<\/h3>\n

4 servings<\/p>\n

Ingredients<\/h3>\n
    \n
  • 1 small chicken, cut in half down the breastbone, and pounded fairly flat (approx 3 lbs.)<\/li>\n
  • Juice of 1 lemon<\/li>\n
  • 1 clove of garlic, crushed<\/li>\n
  • Fresh rosemary, chopped<\/li>\n
  • 3-4 tbsp. olive oil<\/li>\n
  • salt<\/li>\n
  • pepper<\/li>\n<\/ul>\n

    Directions<\/h3>\n

    Rub the chicken with all ingredients and let marinate for several hours. Heat the grill until medium-hot (you should be able to hold your hand 5 in” above the grill for 3 sec).<\/p>\n

    Place chicken, skin side down, and add weight. Cover grill. Grill until dark grill marks appear, approx. 12 minutes. Turn skin side up, re-apply weight and cover, and grill until juices run clear, approx. 15 minutes more.<\/p>\n

    Let rest 10 to 15 minutes before serving.<\/p>\n","protected":false},"excerpt":{"rendered":"
