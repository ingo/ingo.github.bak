Summertime brings with it a delicious wealth of fruits and vegetables. This chutney takes a sweet, fragrant peach and adds just enough heat to keep things interesting.<\/span>
\n
\n[singlepic=4240,300,450]<\/p>\n

Yield<\/h3>\n

2 cups<\/p>\n

Ingredients<\/h3>\n
    \n
  • 1\/2 cup cider vinegar<\/li>\n
  • 1\/2 cup loosely packed brown sugar<\/li>\n
  • 1\/2 cup white sugar<\/li>\n
  • 1\/2 cup sweet red pepper, peeled, seeded, diced 1\/4 inch<\/li>\n
  • 1\/2 cup, small white onion, peeled and diced<\/li>\n
  • 1 small red hot pepper, peeled, seeded and diced<\/li>\n
  • 1 tablespoon finely chopped garlic<\/li>\n
  • 1 tablespoon grated ginger<\/li>\n
  • 1\/2 teaspoon salt<\/li>\n
  • 1 1\/2 pounds firm, fresh peaches, blanched to remove the skin, pit removed, sliced into wedges<\/li>\n<\/ul>\n

    Directions<\/h3>\n

    Put the vinegar and the sugars into a non-reactive pot, place over medium heat and bring to a boil. Add the red peppers, onion, garlic, ginger, salt and simmer 10 minutes. Add the peach segments and simmer an additional 5-10 minutes, or until the peaches are of the desired softness. If a thicker consistency is desired, strain out the solids from the liquid and boil the syrup until thickened. Re-add the peaches.<\/p>\n

    Remove from heat and allow to cool. Serve at room temperature.<\/p>\n

    Notes<\/h3>\n

    If you’d like a little more heat, replace the smaller red pepper with a jalape\u00f1o pepper.<\/p>\n","protected":false},"excerpt":{"rendered":"
