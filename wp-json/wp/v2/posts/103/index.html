This recipe comes via Victor Sodsook. It’s Ingo’s favorite ways to make this dish.<\/span>
\n<\/p>\n

Ingredients<\/h3>\n
    \n
  • 8 oz. dried rice-stick noodles, or 1 16 oz. package fresh (preferred)<\/li>\n
  • 1\/4 lb. medium shrimp<\/li>\n
  • 1\/4 lb. pork tenderloin or bonelss chicken<\/li>\n
  • 4 tbsp. nam pla (fish sauce)<\/li>\n
  • 6 tbsp. distilled white vinegar<\/li>\n
  • 5 tbsp. catsup<\/li>\n
  • 3 tbsp. vegetable oil<\/li>\n
  • 8 cloves garlic, mashed<\/li>\n
  • 2 eggs<\/li>\n
  • 1\/2 chili powder<\/li>\n
  • 6 scallions (including tops), angle-cut into 1 1\/2 in pieces<\/li>\n
  • 1 1\/2 cups bean sprouts<\/li>\n
  • 1\/3 cup roasted unsalted peanuts, crushed<\/li>\n<\/ul>\n

    Directions<\/h3>\n

    If using dried noodles, soak in warm water until soft.<\/p>\n

    Peel and devein the shrimp, but leave the tails on. Slice the pork or chicken into bite-sized strips.<\/p>\n

    In a small bowl, combine the nam pla, vinegar, sugar and catsup. Stir until the sugar is dissolved.<\/p>\n

    Drain the noodles and set aside. Place all the ingedients close to the wok.<\/p>\n

    Set a wok over medium-high heat. When hot, add the oil. Coat the sides. Add the garlic for a few seconds, then add chicken or pork and the shrimp. Stir fry approx. 1 minute.<\/p>\n

    Stir in the sauce mixture and bring to a boil. Add the noodles and toss gently. Stir fry 2 minutes.<\/p>\n

    Break the eggs into the wok. Break up the eggs a bit, then mix them under the noodle mixture. Cook, without stirring for 15 seconds, and then stir fry until all ingredients are well blended.<\/p>\n

    Add chili powder and scallions. Stir fry for 1-2 minutes, then add in bean sprouts and peanuts. Serve immediately<\/p>\n","protected":false},"excerpt":{"rendered":"
