An exceptionally nice eggplant salad (more like a paste) for use on bread or pita. Using canned tomatoes works very well and is nice in wintertime. This one does not contain zucchini and is adapted from a recipe by Claudia Roden.<\/span>
\n<\/p>\n

Yield<\/h3>\n

Serves 6<\/p>\n

Ingredients<\/h3>\n
    \n
  • 1 eggplant (approx 1 1\/2 lbs)<\/li>\n
  • 5 cloves garlic, peeled<\/li>\n
  • 3 large beefsteak tomatoes (ripe), or 2 cups canned crushed tomatoes.<\/li>\n
  • 4 tbsp olive oil<\/li>\n
  • 2 tablespoons wine vinegar (or 1 tbsp white vinegar)<\/li>\n
  • 1\/2 tsp. harissa or 1\/2 tsp. paprika plus a pinch ground chili pepper<\/li>\n
  • 1 tsp. ground cumin<\/li>\n
  • 1\/2 cup chopped flat-leaf parsley<\/li>\n
  • salt<\/li>\n<\/ul>\n

    Directions<\/h3>\n

    Peel the eggplant and chop into cubes. Bring enough water to cover the eggplant (eggplant will float, so approximate) to a boil. Add some salt, garlic and eggplant. Cook until the eggplant is very soft, about 20-30 minutes. Drain well and mash eggplant with a fork.<\/p>\n

    If using whole tomatoes, chop and cook in a pan over low heat until reduced to a sauce, approximately 20 mins. If using canned crushed tomates, reduce this time in half. Mix with the mashed eggplant, the rest of the ingeditents, and salt to taste.<\/p>\n","protected":false},"excerpt":{"rendered":"
