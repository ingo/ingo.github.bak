This recipe was discovered after a search for something to cook in a brand-new Staub cast-iron pan I picked up at a steal from Amazon. The little recipe booklet that comes with the pan has a great many photos of whole chickens being roasted inside, but the recipes all in the end called for chicken parts. Staged photos indeed! Fortunately, I ran across this fantastic recipe from the site Meathenge<\/a> for “Wet Roast Chicken”<\/a> perfect for my cast iron pot. Here is the recipe with minor modifications, and many thanks to Dr. Biggles. We served it with a parsnip-pumpkin puree.<\/span>
<\/p>\n\n\n\n

Roast Chicken with Mushroom Sauce<\/h3>
  • Servings: <\/strong>4<\/li>
  • Print<\/a><\/li><\/ul>
    <\/p>\n\n\n\n

    Ingredients<\/h4><\/p>\n\n\n\n
    • 1 4 lb. roasting chicken<\/li>
    • 1 medium onion, diced<\/li>
    • 2 medium carrots, peeled and cut into 1\/4″ rounds<\/li>
    • 2 stalks celery, diced<\/li>
    • 4 cloves garlic, smashed with the side of a knife<\/li>
    • 3-4 cups chicken stock<\/li>
    • 1\/2 lb. mushrooms, cleaned and chopped in half<\/li>
    • 3 tbsp. olive oil, 2 for pot, 1 for chicken<\/li>
    • 2 tsp. paprika<\/li>
    • salt<\/li><\/ul>\n\n\n\n

      <\/div><\/p>\n\n\n\n

      Directions<\/h4><\/p>\n\n\n\n
      1. Preheat oven to 375 degrees. Place rack on bottom shelf.<\/li>
      2. Remove giblets from chicken. Reserve neck.<\/li>
      3. Cut back out of chicken and cut into several pieces.<\/li>
      4. With breast side down, score the back of the breastbone and flip chicken over.<\/li>
      5. Smack down center of breast in order to flatten chicken somewhat.<\/li>
      6. Heat 2 tbsp. olive oil in 5-6 quart cast iron pot over medium heat.<\/li>
      7. Add onion, celery, carrots, garlic and chicken backs. Saut\u00e9 until vegetables are quite soft, approximately 5-10 minutes.<\/li>
      8. Add enough chicken stock to cover the vegetables and increase heat to high.<\/li>
      9. Bring to a rolling boil and boil 5 minutes.<\/li>
      10. Remove from heat, and place chicken inside pot, breast side up. You basically want the chicken to float on top, barely submerged.<\/li>
      11. Drizzle chicken with olive oil, sprinkle with salt and paprika.<\/li>
      12. Place in oven and roast uncovered until a thermometer inserted in the thickest part of the breast reaches 160 degrees.<\/li>
      13. Remove chicken from oven and pot, and place on a plate to rest. Cover lightly to keep warm.<\/li>
      14. Using a ladle, remove most of the fat floating on top of the liquid. If desired, blend liquid until smooth using an immersion blender.<\/li>
      15. Add mushrooms, and bring sauce to a boil.<\/li>
      16. Simmer for several minutes until mushrooms are cooked through. Serve immediately.<\/li><\/ol>\n\n\n\n
