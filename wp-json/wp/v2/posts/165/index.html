This dish we’ve made multiple times. Sometimes it has turned out wonderful, other times, just good. We are still working on tuning the spice amounts.<\/span>
\n<\/p>\n

Ingredients<\/h3>\n
    \n
  • 1\/4 cup finely chopped onion<\/li>\n
  • 1\/4 tsp. turmeric<\/li>\n
  • 1\/8 tsp. ground cardamom<\/li>\n
  • 1 1\/2 tbsp. unsalted butter<\/li>\n
  • 1\/3 cup long-grain rice<\/li>\n
  • 2\/3 cup chicken broth<\/li>\n
  • 2 tbsp. pistachio nuts or pumpkin seeds, lightly toasted and chopped<\/li>\n
  • 2 tbsp. golden raisins, soaked in boiling water 1 minute and drained<\/li>\n
  • 2 tbsp. thinly sliced green scallions<\/li>\n<\/ul>\n

    Directions<\/h3>\n

    In a small heavy saucepan cook the onion with the turmeric and the cardamom in 1 tbsp. butter over low heat until the onion is softened. Add rice and cook until coated with butter. Ad the broth, bring to a boil, cover, and simmer until liquid is absorbed, approximately 15 minutes.<\/p>\n

    Stir in the pistachios, raisins, scallions, remaining butter and salt and pepper to taste.<\/p>\n","protected":false},"excerpt":{"rendered":"
