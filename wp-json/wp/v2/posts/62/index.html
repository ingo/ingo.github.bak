A very typical southern german recipe, a favorite of Ingo growing up. Great with Hefeknoedel.<\/span>
\n<\/p>\n

Yield<\/h3>\n

4 servings<\/p>\n

Ingredients<\/h3>\n
    \n
  • 3 pounds chuck, cut into 1 \u00bd inch cubes<\/li>\n
  • 1 \u00bd tsp. salt<\/li>\n
  • 1 tsp. ground pepper<\/li>\n
  • 3 tbsp. vegetable oil<\/li>\n
  • 2 medium-large onions, chopped coarse<\/li>\n
  • 3 medium cloves garlic, crushed<\/li>\n
  • 3 cups chicken stock<\/li>\n
  • 2 bay leaves<\/li>\n
  • 1 tsp. dried thyme<\/li>\n
  • 4 large carrots, peeled and sliced into \u00bc inch thick sticks (you want to fish these out later)<\/li>\n
  • 2 stalks celery, sliced into \u00bc in thick sticks (you want to fish these out later)<\/li>\n<\/ul>\n

    For sauce<\/h3>\n
      \n
    • 4 tablespoons butter<\/li>\n
    • 4 tablespoons flour<\/li>\n
    • 2 cups stock (from above recipe)<\/li>\n
    • \u00bd cup dill<\/li>\n
    • \u00be cup sour cream<\/li>\n
    • Salt to taste<\/li>\n
    • Pepper to taste<\/li>\n<\/ul>\n

      Directions<\/h3>\n

      Heat oven to 250 degrees. Place beef cubes in large bowl. Sprinkle with salt and pepper, toss to coat. Heat 2 tablespoons oil over medium high heat in large Dutch oven or pot. Add half of meat and brown on all sides, about 5 minutes. Remove meat and set aside on plate. Repeat process with remaining oil and meat.<\/p>\n

      Add onions to empty Dutch oven and saut\u00e9 until softened, 4 to 5 minutes. Add garlic and continue to cook for 30 seconds. Add stock, bay leaves and thyme and bring to a simmer. Add meat and return to a simmer. Cover and place pot in oven. Cook for 1 hour.<\/p>\n

      Remove pot from oven and add carrots and celery. Cover and return to oven. Cook just until meat is tender, 1 \u00bd to 2 hours.<\/p>\n

      Remove pot from oven.<\/p>\n

      Remove 2 cups stock from pot. Set aside.<\/p>\n

      Melt butter on medium heat in heavy-bottomed saucepan. When butter is melted and foam has subsided, pull pan off heat and add flour, stirring briskly. Return to heat, and stir constantly, 3-6 minutes until golden brown.<\/p>\n

      Pull pan off heat and quickly whisk in \u00bd of stock. When mixture is smooth, add in remaining stock. Cook 3-4 minutes.<\/p>\n

      Remove pan from heat. Mix in sour cream, dill, salt and pepper to taste.<\/p>\n

      Remove meat from pot and add to sauce. Server with Hefeknoedel or soft bread.<\/p>\n","protected":false},"excerpt":{"rendered":"
